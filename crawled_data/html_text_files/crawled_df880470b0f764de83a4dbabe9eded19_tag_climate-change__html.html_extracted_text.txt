What is the carbon cycle?
All living things contain carbon and the carbon cycle is the process through which the element continuously moves from one place in nature to another. As our planet is a closed system, the overall amount of carbon doesnâ€™t change. However, the level of carbon stored in a particular reservoir, including the atmosphere, can and does change, as does the speed at which carbon moves from one reservoir to another.

CO

From this cycle, carbon is returned to the atmosphere through, for example, volcanic eruptions and the weathering of rocks.
